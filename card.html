Hedgehog

Hedgehogs have about 5,000 to 7,000 spines. Each spine falls out after about a year and a replacement grows. They are solitary creatures.

  • Scientific Name: Erinaceinae
  • Diet: Not exclusively insectivore but almost omnivorous
  • Behavior: Primarily nocturnal
  • Defense: Roll into a tight spiny ball

There are 17 species of hedgehog in Europe, Asia and Africa, and in New Zealand by introduction. Headgehogs are known as the gardener's friend, since they prey on many common garden pests. They are actually considered as wild animals and it is illegal to own a headgehog in some U.S. cities and states.
